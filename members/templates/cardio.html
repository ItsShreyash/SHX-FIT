HIIT

  • HIIT involves alternating between short bursts of intense exercise and brief rest or lower-intensity periods
  • Examples include sprinting, cycling at maximum effort, or performing high-intensity bodyweight exercises like burpees or jumping lunges
  • HIIT workouts are typically shorter in duration but highly effective for burning calories and improving cardiovascular fitness
  • They can be adapted to various fitness levels and goals, making them suitable for beginners to advanced athletes
  • HIIT is known for its ability to increase metabolism, improve insulin sensitivity, and promote fat loss while preserving muscle mass

LISS

  • LISS involves maintaining a consistent, low-intensity pace for an extended period
  • Common forms include walking, leisurely cycling, swimming, or using cardio machines like the treadmill or elliptical at a moderate pace
  • LISS is effective for improving cardiovascular health, enhancing recovery, and burning calories
  • It's particularly suitable for individuals looking for a low-impact, sustainable form of cardio or as active recovery on rest day
  • LISS sessions can vary in duration and intensity based on fitness goals and preferences

Circuit Training

  • Cardio circuit training combines cardiovascular exercises with strength exercises in a circuit format
  • This approach involves performing a series of exercises back-to-back with minimal rest in between, alternating between cardio and strength movements
  • Examples include jump rope intervals, kettlebell swings, bodyweight exercises like mountain climbers or high knees, followed by strength exercises like squats, lunges, or push-ups
  • Cardio circuit training provides a comprehensive full-body workout, effectively burning calories, improving cardiovascular fitness, and building strength simultaneously
  • It offers variety and can be customized based on individual fitness goals and preferences, making it suitable for those seeking a dynamic and efficient workout routine
